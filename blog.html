Strangers that came to town

  • Ambrose Flack
  • April 1, 1980

"Strangers that came to town", a novel by Ambrose Flack, published in 1980, a short story of an immigrant family moving to, and rejected by the natives there for how they dressed and their hidden personality. A story showing how even as big as a family could be dircriminated by a larger group of people. How parents' action could influence their kids and be relfected upon, teaching us not to be bad ones. As many as parallels in between this and Martin Luther King's speech, I'll be talking about that in this topic.

All speeches given has a singular purpose and King's speech was deliberately emphasing on the topic freedom and equality for his race. As how the Duvitches' family fought for theirs, in the story. The Duvitche's family has been discriminated and treated lowly by the natives there, them not knowing nor caring about what kinds of problems they must have endured and assuming them hold-backs for them, treating them,their whole family, unequally for many years. It was really hurtful to see how the childrens even were treated that way in their early childhood, made me think how it would affect their growth and understanding of the society. A lot of negative possibilities came into my mind, made me realised Matrin luther King's acts were of very neccessary and crucial for the growth as a country, without this america would not stand where they are now, I can see. The power of unity and love he seeded is now budding into an elegant flower with variant colors that is now visible, such a sight for sore eyes.

In his speech included many unequal actions, the whole race being mistreated and shows how strongly he feels about it and being able to speak out meant a lot for him and for this people, a hero not just for his race but for all the people existing till now. Not without this unity as a whole he created, humanity could not have gotten this far, but of course this won't just be a good cause there sure is to be bad ones too, but it is now our mission to carry on his good will and help his efforts not to be in vain."I would be fatal for the nation to overlook the urgency of the moment and to underestimate the determination of it’s colored citizens." (Martin_Luther_King_Jr) People should never be treated that way like in the Strangers that came to town story and you now have seen both sides of the action and I think youn already know a better result.

"We can never be satisfied as long as our children are stripped of their selfhood and robbed of their dignity by signs stating “for white only.”(Martin_Luther_King_Jr). As how the Duvitches children were being left out and laught at for wearing different clothes and having different meals, these actions arent acceptable even as a kid, it is their parents' duty to fix it and kids should never the word dircimination ever again after all of Dr.King's efforts as an American citizen.

I believe you have enough ideas of the topics of both Kind's speech and the themes of the story, so let me conclude this with my favorite quote of Dr.King's speech, "I have a dream that one day down in Alabama, with its vicious racists, with its governor having his lips dripping with the words of interposition and nullification; that one day right down in Alabama little black boys and black girls will be able to join hands with little white boys and white girls as sisters and brothers.", a dream that now have come true, a very beautiful although there are still obstacles in this journey i am willing to walk it and very inspired by King's journey.
