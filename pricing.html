Stranger That Came to Town

  1. Home
  2. STCTT

There seems to have two major themes the author presented in this short story, strangers that came to town, one of which is the importance of freedom and the other specifically stating to not judge a book by its cover.

The story started off gloomy, chilly vibe where the author introduces us, readers, with the characters and the arrival of new blood into the town, Duvitches family, where they get looked down upon by the most well educated family in the town which then led to be discriminated by the whole. So they didn’t get good first impression because they were immigrants and first of their nationality to ever settle in this neighborhood, ” poverty, amid prosperity is often embarrassing and irritating to the prosperous” and were marked people even the little duvitches were treated differently by their peers for eating different lunches and dressed differently. The start of the story was really frustrating to read seeing how these people didn’t give them a chance to show the good sides of them and judging people too quickly because they were different from them and too easily influenced by the leading family, not deciding for themselves. But it was really satisfying and a relief to know that the father was a dignified respectable character in the story, the author showed really good on describing the fury of the dad like a true beast yet respectable how Tom and the mc obeyed their dad’s will and showed what they were capable of with unwavering will at the pond incident.

Although, the story emphasizes on showing how freedom changes and bring out the best in a person/group it seemed to me that it was about to not judge a book by its cover, while reading it. It’s obvious how the author build up from intro to climax, which I believe is the pond incident, to show the difference people can make and be better if treated correctly and respected accordingly. Even the old frail Mrs.duvitches seemed to have the knit for nursing and extended knowledge of medical fields.

I still think the main theme better suits with the latter statement “Not judging the book by its cover”. After the pond incident where the boys paid back for what they did by presenting the duvitches with their hard gained fishes, they have certainly gained back their dignity and respect of the duvitches and they start to see the best side them which surprised the well-educated dad. That’s when he realized that his former impressions of the duvitches are completely opposite of what it actually is “Who would have dreamed that the Duvitches would have so much more to offer us than we have to offer them?" .

Work cited 1. Flack , Ambrose, The Strangers that Came to Town 1953.
