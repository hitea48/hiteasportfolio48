“I have a dream” a speech delivered by Martin Luther King in 1963 a noon on the 28th of August which shaken up the American society, that a man finally spoke up for the equality he deserved. As heated as the day might have been his words filled with emotions must have flared up the freezing hearts of thousands and thousands of audience infront of this single man speaking about his rights. A truly remarkable day it was, and taught the later generations, us, many lessons.

From the start till the end his speech was perfect, not for choice of words or how convinceful that he was but for his bravery and ability to love. Many before him and with him must’ve felt the same as him about how they were being mistreated but not a single made a significant change that would’ve lasted till this day. His love as a father and for his race must’ve fueled him to reach this far and that’s something to be recognized and be respected. “This momentous decree came as a great beacon of hope to millions of slaves, who had been seared in the flames of whithering injustice.”(King). Out of millions of victims in a century, finally came fort Martin Luther King, a spokeman whos brave enough to face the whitemen society and with enough cards to play out for it.

A total of 1667 words delivered in 16 minutes of time, I rekon, every single words must've flowed into those many audiences' hearts that he had as it did to me watching a video record."I have a dream that my four children will one day live in a nation where they will not be judged by the color of their skin, but by the content of their character" Martin Luther King Jr, 28 August 1963. A represnetation as a father of his whole race, felt it in my heart the power of his voice, a very strong and reliable voice, which we can somehow relate with our situation in Myanmar(Burma) and hoping we could have similar ending as they did.
